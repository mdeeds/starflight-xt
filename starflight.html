Net force = SUM(F_i) = d(m v_cm) / dt
That is, sum up the individual force vectors. Divide by mass, and you have change in velocity at the center of mass.
The inertia of a cylinder is m r^2 / 4 + m l^2 / 12. r is the radius, l is the length, it rotates end over end.
Net torque = SUM(r_i x F_i) = d(I_cm omega) / dt
Sum the cross of the force with a radius to the center of mass. Divide by inertia, and you have change in angular velocity.
